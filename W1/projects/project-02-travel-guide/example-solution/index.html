Paris Travel Guide

The City of Light - Where history, art, and romance meet

Welcome to Paris

Paris is the capital city of France and one of the most visited cities in the world. Located in northern France along the Seine River, Paris is home to approximately 2.1 million people in the city center and over 12 million in the greater metropolitan area.

Known as the "City of Light" (La Ville Lumière), Paris earned this nickname because it was one of the first cities to use gas street lighting on a large scale. Today, it continues to shine as a global center for art, fashion, gastronomy, and culture.

Whether you're wandering through world-class museums, enjoying a croissant at a sidewalk café, or watching the Eiffel Tower sparkle at night, Paris offers unforgettable experiences at every turn.

Top 5 Must-See Attractions

These are the essential places every visitor should experience. I've ranked them based on popularity and how much they represent the spirit of Paris.

  1. The Eiffel Tower - The iconic iron lattice tower built in 1889. You can take an elevator or stairs to the top for panoramic views of Paris. Best visited at sunset when it sparkles with lights for 5 minutes every hour. (Official website)
  2. The Louvre Museum - The world's largest art museum and home to the Mona Lisa, Venus de Milo, and thousands of other masterpieces. Plan at least half a day here. (Official website)
  3. Notre-Dame Cathedral - A masterpiece of French Gothic architecture dating back to 1163. Though damaged by fire in 2019, it remains an iconic symbol of Paris and French history. (Official website)
  4. Montmartre and Sacré-Cœur - A charming hilltop neighborhood known for its artistic history (Picasso and Van Gogh lived here!). The white-domed basilica offers stunning views of the city.
  5. Champs-Élysées and Arc de Triomphe - Paris's most famous avenue stretches from the Place de la Concorde to the Arc de Triomphe. Climb to the top of the arch for another spectacular viewpoint. (Arc de Triomphe website)

French Food You Must Try

French cuisine is famous worldwide, and Paris is the perfect place to experience it. Here are some local specialties you shouldn't miss:

  • Croissants - Flaky, buttery pastry that's perfect for breakfast. Try them fresh from a boulangerie (bakery) in the morning when they're still warm.
  • Baguettes - The iconic French bread with a crispy crust and soft interior. Fun fact: by law, a "traditional" baguette can only contain flour, water, salt, and yeast!
  • French Onion Soup (Soupe à l'Oignon) - A rich beef broth with caramelized onions, topped with crusty bread and melted Gruyère cheese.
  • Crêpes - Thin pancakes that can be sweet (with Nutella, fruit, or sugar) or savory (with ham, cheese, and egg). Find them at crêperies throughout the city.
  • Macarons - Delicate almond cookies sandwiched with flavored cream. Ladurée and Pierre Hermé are famous for these colorful treats.
  • Escargot - Snails cooked in garlic butter and herbs. A classic French dish if you're feeling adventurous!

Essential Travel Tips

Make your Paris trip smoother with these helpful tips:

  • Best time to visit: April to June and September to October offer pleasant weather and fewer crowds. July and August are peak tourist season.
  • Getting around: The Métro (subway) is the easiest way to travel. Buy a Paris Visite pass for unlimited travel, or use contactless payment.
  • Money: France uses the Euro (€). Credit cards are widely accepted, but carry some cash for small shops and markets.
  • Language: French is the official language. Learn a few basic phrases like "Bonjour" (hello), "Merci" (thank you), and "Parlez-vous anglais?" (do you speak English?).
  • Museum passes: The Paris Museum Pass gives skip-the-line access to many museums and monuments. Buy it online or at participating locations.
  • Dining etiquette: Meals are meant to be enjoyed slowly. Don't rush, and remember that tipping is included in the bill (service compris), though small tips are appreciated for excellent service.

Hidden Gems

Beyond the famous landmarks, Paris has many lesser-known treasures worth discovering:

  1. Canal Saint-Martin - A picturesque canal in the 10th arrondissement with iron footbridges, trendy cafés, and a relaxed atmosphere. Great for a peaceful walk.
  2. Rue Crémieux - A charming pedestrian street lined with colorful pastel-painted houses. Perfect for photos and a quiet stroll.
  3. Promenade Plantée - An elevated park built on an old railway line, inspiring New York's High Line. Beautiful in spring and summer.
